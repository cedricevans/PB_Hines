Our Origin

A Legacy Rooted in Faith & Determination

We trace our history to Liberty County, Georgia and the Sand Hills Plantation. On August 16, 1845, Pompey B. Hines was born into slavery. Upon his death on July 5, 1919, our father Pompey B. Hines had acquired more than 450 acres of land.

He left a rich legacy as the father of 17 children. Today there are more than 800 living descendants of Pompey B. Hines. We are still searching for words that will express our gratitude.

Our family is like an acorn left unattended in a field of tall grass trampled on and moved around a bit. The winter was harsh and yet it endured. Now its spring: a root sprouts, a stem appears and reaches for the sky. A tree is born. Not just any tree an oak tree.

Pompey was born a slave. After the Civil War, life was unsettling, and the 1870 Census revealed him in Willie, Ga. a small farming community in Liberty County. This is where he put his roots down. This is where our family tree begins to grow.

Biographies of the 17 Descendants

Theodore Ball Family Tree 1 of 17 Born Mar. 15, 1874 · Died March 1943

Theodore Ball is the 1st child of Pompey B. Hines, born in Liberty County Georgia and died in Evans County Georgia. His mother is Delia Ball.

Theodore owned more than 600 acres of land in Evans County; he was a farmer as well as an entrepreneur, owning several naval stores and a Turpentine Still that produced rosin.

He was a member of Jerusalem Baptist Church, serving as Secretary, Deacon and Trustee. In 1941 when the Federal Government seized his land and the land of Jerusalem Baptist Church, he served on the committee for relocating the church from Liberty County to its present location in Bryan County Georgia.

Theodore Ball married Sarah Barnett, producing 3 children: Ludelia, Ollie, and Richard (Bud) Ball. After Sarah's death he married Buelah Holland, this union produced 14 children, 7 of whom died in infancy. The 7 children who lived are: Joe T., Avis, W. Hardin, Milla Nease, Overidia, Gwendolyn, and Theddeus.

He was 68 years old at time of death.

Arlene Hines Johnson Family Tree 2 of 17 Born Apr. 23, 1874 · Died Dec. 10, 1960

Arlene Hines was born in Liberty County Georgia and died in Pembroke, Ga. She was the first child born to Ellen and Pompey B. Hines.

Arlene married Samuel Johnson on Feb. 11, 1898 likely a church wedding, as the marriage license was signed by Rev. S. M. Walker, Pastor of Jerusalem Baptist Church. Brothers Samuel and Houston Johnson felt fortunate when they came upon the Pompey B. Hines household and found it full of fine women. They chose the two oldest Arlene and Bessie. The two brothers married the two sisters, using the same pastor, three years apart.

The newly weds moved to Groveland, Ga. where they became farmers. This marriage did not produce any children, but they were fortunate enough to raise two girls: Annalyzer and Alzader.

She lived to be 95 years old longer than any of her siblings.

Julian H. Hines Family Tree 3 of 17 Born Nov. 7, 1875 · Died Nov. 19, 1967

Julian H. Hines was born in Liberty County Georgia and died in Bulloch County Georgia. He stood 6' tall, very wiry, medium complexion, with an uncompromising demeanor. He was the 1st boy born to Ellen and Pompey B. Hines.

Julian was Pompey Hines' Lieutenant the one person Pompey could count on to get the job done. His brother Doyle said "if papa left in the morning and told Bulb (Julian's nickname) to stand by the gate until he returned, then Bulb would still be standing there when Papa returned that night." His siblings did not challenge him; they gave him all the respect a big brother deserved.

Julian married Christchaue George on Dec. 22, 1903, and this marriage produced 9 children. In 1941 the Federal Government seized his land in Liberty County for military use. The family moved to Bulloch County Georgia, where Julian purchased 205 acres from H.C. Burney and turned a small farm into a big, successful farming operation.

He was a devoted Christian and Chairman of the Deacon Board at Jerusalem Baptist Church. He was 92 years old at time of death.

Bessie Hines Johnson Family Tree 4 of 17 Born Apr. 17, 1876 · Died Nov. 1964

Bessie Hines was born in Liberty County Georgia and died in Pembroke, Ga. She was the 2nd girl born to Ellen and Pompey Hines. Most of the children were light brown in complexion, but Bessie had a very light complexion with long black curly hair.

When someone asked her brother Doyle "is Bessie the prettiest of all your sisters?" Doyle replied, "at least she thinks that she is the prettiest."

Bessie married Houston Johnson on May 12, 1901. She was ahead of her time a socialite even though there was poverty all around her. Her furniture was top of the line; her clothing was far beyond what the women of her community were wearing. When everyone came to church on Sunday riding in old wooden wagons pulled by mules, Bessie came in a buggy with leather upholstery pulled by a horse. She was always immaculately dressed, and it was said she once vacationed in Florida with Madame Walker.

She was 88 years old at time of death.

Delburgh Hines Family Tree 5 of 17 Born Jun. 17, 1879 · Died Jan. 7, 1901

Delburgh Hines was born in Liberty County Georgia and died there at the age of 22. He was the 4th child of Ellen and Pompey Hines. It is thought he died of fever. He never married and had no children.

Abbie Hines Clanton Family Tree 6 of 17 Born Nov. 12, 1881 · Died Feb. 28, 1963

Abbie Hines was born in Liberty County Georgia and died in Pembroke, Ga. She was a person of medium height and weight, very quiet but extremely focused. She was 2nd in command among her siblings, next to Julian Hines.

When her mother Ellen died in 1900, only one of the 16 children was married, and there were 12 children under 19 years old five of them under 6. Abbie became their surrogate mother.

Abbie married Julian H. Clanton on March 11, 1905. The newly weds moved to Pembroke, Ga., where all 11 of their children were born. Abbie and Hardrick Clanton donated approximately 10 acres of land to the Bryan County Board of Education for the purpose of building a school for Black children. This land was occupied by Pembroke High School until the mid-1960s.

"My mother, Esther Hines Simmons, told me she would hate to see her sister Abbie come home. After she got married she would come home and stay for a week or more washing dirty clothes, cleaning house and making quilts... we would be glad to see her leave." Lillie Simmons Upshaw

She was a devoted, lifelong member of Jerusalem Baptist Church. She was 82 years old at time of death.

Annie Hines Levant Family Tree 7 of 17 Born Jan. 1, 1884 · Died Apr. 12, 1973

Annie Hines was born in Liberty County Georgia. She was the 6th child and 4th girl in the family of 16. She was a person of medium height and weight with a mild disposition.

On Dec. 28, 1902, she and Emmitt Levant were joined in Holy Matrimony. Annie and Emmitt farmed various locations until her father Pompey B. Hines died in 1919, when she inherited her share of the 450 acres. Their 10 children were born on this farm.

In 1941, the Federal Government seized their land for military use. Good fortune smiled upon the family Emmitt learned of a loan a Claxton Bank was about to call in, and was told that if he could pay off the remaining balance, approximately $800.00, he could have a 230-acre farm across the river in Evans County, Georgia.

"Mama and Daddy was so happy to get the farm, they thought this was a very good deal, so good that mama supposedly tipped the banker." Fletcher Levant

A devoted, lifelong member of Jerusalem Baptist Church. She was 89 years old at time of death.

Edward Hines Family Tree 8 of 17 Born 1885 · Died Sept. 15, 1952

Edward Hines was born in Liberty County Georgia and died in Savannah, Ga. He stood 6' tall, medium weight, light brown complexion, and very mild mannered.

It was said that Frank was the most handsome of all the boys, and Edward was a close second both said to be "a lady's man." Edward married Daisy, producing 3 children: Charles, Winton, and Jewell. He later divorced and married Geneva Hall on Apr. 3, 1927, producing one more child, Edward Pompey Hines.

Edward moved to Savannah, Ga., where he reunited with his brothers Johnny and Pompey H. He worked as a gardener in the Savannah area.

James E. Hines, Sr. Family Tree 9 of 17 Born Sept. 22, 1887 · Died Jul. 26, 1965

James E. Hines, Sr. was born in Liberty County Georgia and died in Pembroke, Ga. He stood about 6' tall and carried about 175 lbs on his wiry frame medium complexion and mild mannered. Everyone called him "Jim."

James married Etner Johnson on Feb. 4, 1917 in Liberty County Georgia, he was 30 years old.

"Let me tell you the story of how mother and daddy meet. Daddy drove his horse and buggy to the Johnson's house to see Aunt Katie but Aunt Katie had already left to go to a party with another fellow, my Daddy was never one to be out done so he asked my Mother if she would like to go for a ride on his buggy, she accepted." Effie Dell Hines

Jim possessed all the family traits the willingness to work hard, the dogmatic determination to succeed plus one more: the desire to be first. He was the first of his siblings to place education as a top priority. At a time when 5–6 years of Elementary Schooling was considered more than adequate, Jim enrolled all 3 of his children into prestigious Dorchester Academy. James E. Hines, Jr. was the 1st descendant of Pompey B. Hines to graduate from high school and the 1st to enter college.

He was 78 years old at time of death.

Samuel Hines Family Tree 10 of 17 Born 1888 · Died Sept. 2, 1921

Samuel Hines was born in Liberty County Georgia. It has been speculated that he served in the Army. He never married and has no children.

Walter Hines Family Tree 11 of 17 Born Aug. 10, 1889 · Died Apr. 24, 1980

Walter Hines was born in Liberty County Georgia and died in Philadelphia, Pa. He stood about 6' tall, weighed about 200 lbs, and was very mild mannered.

On Oct. 28, 1919, Walter married Esther Williams in Liberty County, Ga., producing 2 children. The newly weds had a serious disagreement and Esther moved to Philadelphia where she had family. Around 1925, Walter reunited with his wife in Philadelphia.

For years the family was unsure of his whereabouts. He was not even present to sign the deed that sold his share of the land to the Federal Government. Then, in April 1964 after being away 39 years he finally came home to his sister Bessie Hines Johnson's funeral. It was said that Bessie was his favorite sister. It was a funeral, but everyone was happy that Walter had finally come home.

He was 91 years old at time of death.

Christina Hines Mack Family Tree 12 of 17 Born Jun. 5, 1892 · Died Apr. 12, 1982

Christina Hines was born in Liberty County Georgia and died in Pembroke, Ga. She was the 11th child and 5th girl in the family of 18 children. She was a small person with brown complexion and long curly hair.

Christina Hines married Joe Mack on Jan. 8, 1922 in Liberty County Georgia she was 30 years old. Pastor S. M. Walker of Jerusalem Baptist Church signed the marriage license, making it likely a church celebration.

Early 1940, Joe Mack purchased approximately 25 acres of land in Groveland, Ga., where Christina and Joe reared their 3 children: Leroy Hines, Orlando Mack, and Alexander Mack.

She was a devoted, lifelong member of Jerusalem Baptist Church. She was 90 years old at time of death.

Frank Hines Family Tree 13 of 17 Born 1894 · Died Dec. 1930

Frank Hines was born in Liberty County Georgia and died in Waycross, Ga. He was the 12th child and 7th son. He is said to have been the most handsome of all the boys well liked by siblings, neighbors and friends. When Frank came home, young ladies would come from everywhere and frolic at the "Big House." Frank never married.

As a young man Frank ventured away from the family to establish his own identity, moving to Waycross, Ga. He was killed there by two men who robbed him of approximately $45.00. He was 36 years old.

His brothers, led by Julian Hines, went to Waycross and claimed his body. He was laid to rest at Jerusalem Baptist Church in Liberty County Georgia.

Doyle L. Hines, Sr. Family Tree 14 of 17 Born Sept. 14, 1895 · Died Feb. 2, 1989

Doyle L. Hines was born in Liberty County Georgia and died in Pembroke, Ga. He stood about 5'9" and weighed 180 lbs. He was the 13th child born to Ellen and Pompey B. Hines.

Doyle was drafted into the Army during WWI, trained as a cook. He was standing on the depot in Chamblee, Ga. waiting for a troop train when he received word that Germany had surrendered and the War was over. His orders were cancelled and he returned to the farm in Liberty County.

On June 12, 1927, Doyle married Celia Strickland she was 18 and Doyle was 32. The newly weds moved into the "Big House" where 7 children were born. When the Federal Government seized the farm in 1941, Doyle moved his family to Bryan County, sharecropping for 2 years before purchasing a farm in Pembroke, Ga. from Dr. W.E. Smith on credit.

He was forever grateful to Dr. Smith for his trust. That farm still remains in the family today.

He was 93 years old at time of death.

Esther Hines Simmons Family Tree 15 of 17 Born Sept. 14, 1897 · Died Oct. 22, 1980

Esther Hines was born in Liberty County Georgia and died in Tuskegee, Ala. She was the 14th child and baby girl born to Ellen and Pompey B. Hines. She stood about 5'5", medium weight.

She was 3 years old when her mother died of fever in 1900. Her father decided she would be the first person in the family to attend high school, enrolling her in the prestigious Dorchester Academy in Liberty County Georgia operated by the AME Church, as the State of Georgia provided only Elementary Education for Black children at that time.

Esther married Purley Simmons Feb. 13, 1916, producing 7 children. In November 1926, Purley was injured in an automobile accident that left him paralyzed from the neck down. As a Veteran, he was sent to the only VA hospital treating Black patients at that time Tuskegee, Ala.

Esther moved her 7 children to Tuskegee and in doing so, moved her family to the Mecca of Black education. She bought land, built a house, cared for her husband, and raised her children. All of them graduated from Tuskegee High School. Her daughter Mattye Lenora was the first person in the family to receive a college degree a Bachelor of Science from Tuskegee in 1947.

She was 83 years old at time of death.

Pompey H. Hines Family Tree 16 of 17 Born Mar. 20, 1899 · Died May 7, 1965

Pompey H. Hines was born in Liberty County Georgia and died in Boston, Mass. He stood about 5'9" and weighed about 180 lbs. He was 21 months old when his mother died, raised by his father, older sisters, and brothers.

He was a pipe fitter, house painter, cement finisher, glass cutter, brick mason, and carpenter. He and his father, Pompey B. Hines, were of dark complexion but unlike his tall, lanky father, Pompey H. was slight, and he had something his father did not: charisma. All of his brothers and sisters adored him. Someone gave him the nickname "Doc" when he was young, and this is what everyone called him. He was loved from Willie, Ga. to Boston, Mass. and beyond.

He married Ada Douglass on Dec. 31, 1922. Their first and only child, Elizabeth, was born at the "Big House" on Apr. 8, 1923. The family eventually settled in Savannah, Ga. around 1938. Ada died, leaving Pompey H. to raise his only child. Elizabeth later married Everette Sheppard in 1952 and moved to Boston; her father later joined them there.

He was 66 years old at time of death.

Johnny Hines Family Tree 17 of 17 Born Jul. 8, 1900 · Died Jul. 16, 1958

Johnny Hines was born in Liberty County Georgia and died in Savannah, Ga. He was the baby of the family of 16 children. He stood about 5'10", medium weight, with a large mustache. Pompey B. Hines was 55 years old when Johnny was born.

Johnny inherited the "Big House" upon the death of his father but never took possession of the property, allowing his brothers and sisters to live there. He separated himself from the family for about 20 years, working on vegetable farms at various locations in Florida.

It was in Jacksonville, Fla., where he befriended a man who later became gravely ill. The gentleman asked Johnny to "see after" his two young daughters. One of the daughters was named Rose; in 1939 Johnny married Rose she was 19 years old and he was 39. The newly weds moved to Savannah, Ga., where their 3 children were born.

He was 58 years old at time of death.

Yet we have one thing in common we are the descendants of Pompey B. Hines. We are one. We are Family." Bud Hines
A Legacy of Learning

Education: The Key to Prosperity

Examining all of the documents of land purchases, Pompey B. Hines' signature on those documents was that of an X. It is not a great revelation that Pompey B. Hines could not read nor write the slave master did not have a need for an educated slave. Despite this handicap he became a successful farmer, and somewhere along the way he learned to count money. At the time of his death in 1919, his estate was conservatively valued at $7,731.12.

Pompey realized that education is the key to prosperity. At a time when 5–6 years of schooling was a sufficient education for Black children, Pompey decided that his baby girl would attend high school the prestigious Dorchester Academy. In the early 1900s, the State of Georgia did not provide high school education for Black children; the AME Church operated just two high schools in the state.

In a short span of 50 years, the descendants of Pompey B. Hines have obtained more than 150 college degrees. Each succeeding generation has raised the bar, inspired by those who came before.

Mattye Lenora Simmons BS Tuskegee University, 1947 was the first descendant of Pompey B. Hines to receive a college degree.
